Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city on the island of Honshu. It's famous for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It's also known for its formal traditions such as kaiseki dining, consisting of multiple courses of precise dishes, and geisha female entertainers often found in the Gion district.

  1. Kinkaku-ji - The Golden Pavilion, a stunning Zen Buddhist temple.
  2. Fushimi Inari-taisha - Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - A picturesque bamboo forest.
  4. Gion District - The famous geisha district with traditional wooden machiya houses.
  5. Nijo Castle - A flatland castle with beautiful gardens and historical significance.
  • Kaiseki - A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner.
  • Yudofu - Hot tofu, a specialty in Kyoto's colder months.
  • Matcha - Powdered green tea, often served in traditional tea ceremonies.
  • Soba - Buckwheat noodles, often enjoyed cold with dipping sauce.
  • Yatsuhashi - A traditional sweet made from glutinous rice flour, often filled with red bean paste.
